Review of Better Call Saul

Tristan Knott

January 30, 2023

Better Call Saul is a standout show that continues to impress viewers with its outstanding writing, exceptional acting, and masterful storytelling. The prequel to Breaking Bad, the show focuses on the transformation of Jimmy McGill into the criminal lawyer Saul Goodman, offering a compelling look at the character's journey. Bob Odenkirk delivers a tour-de-force performance as Jimmy/Saul, bringing depth and nuance to the character and making viewers care about his fate.

The show's writing is top-notch, with well-crafted storylines that are both engaging and thought-provoking. The balance of humor and drama is handled perfectly, creating a unique viewing experience that is both entertaining and thought-provoking. The show's gradual transformation of Jimmy into Saul is handled with care, allowing audiences to understand and empathize with the character's motivations. The supporting cast is equally impressive, with standout performances from Jonathan Banks as Mike Ehrmantraut, Rhea Seehorn as Kim Wexler, and Michael Mando as Nacho Varga.

The show's production values are also top-notch, with outstanding cinematography, attention to detail, and a richly realized Albuquerque setting. The show's use of music is especially noteworthy, with a standout soundtrack that perfectly complements each scene. Overall, Better Call Saul is a must-watch for fans of Breaking Bad and anyone who appreciates top-quality television. Its blend of humor, drama, and exceptional storytelling make it one of the best shows on television today.

Better Call Saul Trivia!

  • Trivia #1: Bob Odenkirk initially turned down reprising his role as Saul Goodman as he felt Breaking Bad was fulfilling enough and he wanted to spend time with his kids. His kids assured him they'll be ok and encouraged him to come back.

  • Trivia #2: Better Call Saul scored the second highest debut ratings in cable history, with an overall 6.9 million viewers for its pilot episode on AMC. Fellow AMC series Fear the Walking Dead (2015) surpassed that record with 10.1 million viewers in its first episode.

  • Trivia #3: All the episode titles in Season 1 end in the letter "O" except Better Call Saul: Alpine Shepherd Boy (2015). It was originally titled "Jello," but they were not allowed to use that trademarked name.
